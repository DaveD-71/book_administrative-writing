Do not convert this note into points unless a separate grading policy is added later.

## How To Use This Note

- Use the same six criteria as the learner-facing rubric so self-review and external review stay aligned.
- Judge the portfolio as a set of four linked pieces, not as four unrelated texts.
- Reward clear fit to purpose, audience, and document type rather than decorative language.
- Keep the intermediate level in view. A strong portfolio should be clear, controlled, and professional without sounding unnaturally advanced.

## Analytic Marking Note

| Criterion | Ready | Nearly ready | Not yet ready | Evidence prompt |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Task completion | All four required items are present, and each piece clearly fits its task and document type. | Most required items are present and usable, but one item is weakly matched or incomplete. | One or more required items are missing, misread, or poorly matched to the task. | Does the portfolio contain the right set of documents, and does each one fit its communicative purpose? |
| Audience and tone | Reader relationship is clear, and tone stays professional and appropriate across the portfolio. | Tone is mostly appropriate, but one or two pieces drift or under-adapt to the reader. | Tone is too direct, vague, inconsistent, or mismatched to the audience in important places. | Does each text sound right for its intended reader? |
| Clarity and organization | Each document has a clear main point, logical order, and easy-to-follow structure. | Overall meaning is clear, but some pieces are uneven, loosely organized, or harder to follow than they should be. | One or more pieces are confusing, weakly structured, or poorly sequenced. | Is each document easy to follow from beginning to end? |
| Accuracy and control | Grammar, sentence control, and basic mechanics are accurate enough to support confident workplace reading. | Meaning is mostly clear, but recurring language-control errors still weaken one or more pieces. | Frequent errors distract the reader or weaken professional usability. | Is the language controlled enough for routine workplace communication? |
| Key information and next steps | The portfolio includes the needed background, main point, and next action or outcome where appropriate. | Most pieces include the needed information, but one or two still leave an important gap. | Key information is missing, too vague, or not shaped to the task. | Does each document give the reader the information they actually need? |
| Consistency and revision quality | Revised pieces clearly improve on earlier drafts, and the full portfolio maintains a consistent professional style. | Revision is visible, but some improvements are partial or the portfolio style is uneven. | Revision is weak or unclear, and the portfolio still feels inconsistent across pieces. | Do the revised documents show meaningful improvement and a stable overall style? |

## Overall Judgment

| Overall judgment | Select one | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Portfolio readiness | Ready / Nearly ready / Not yet ready | |

Judgment guidance:

- `Ready`: the portfolio is clearly usable as a final intermediate submission and shows stable control across the major criteria.
- `Nearly ready`: the portfolio is broadly successful but still needs one or two focused revisions before final sign-off.
- `Not yet ready`: important gaps remain in task completion, audience control, clarity, accuracy, key information, or revision quality.
