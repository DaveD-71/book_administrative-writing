### 6. Module 2 remains over-clustered around trilateral and counterpart-office coordination scenarios

**Category:** Prompt And Task Design / Consistency And Cohesion  
**Criteria:** `5B.1 Level appropriacy` / `5D.3 Calibration` / `5D.4 Choice-list quality` / `5E.3 Variety without chaos`  
**Result:** `Revise`

Evidence from `adv/md/n10/aw-adv_mod2_n10.md`:

- Unit 4 `H3` JPO option:
  - trilateral examiner-exchange planning with multiple unresolved decision points
- Unit 5 `H3` JPO option:
  - terminology and procedure mismatch in a shared examination document
- Unit 6 `E1` JPO option:
  - classification inconsistency in a joint search report prepared for trilateral circulation
- Unit 6 `H3` JPO option:
  - shared guidance note for trilateral search coordination with review-sequence conflict
- Unit 7 `E1` JPO option:
  - contribution to an upcoming trilateral technical meeting with unresolved points before drafting
- Unit 7 `H3` JPO option:
  - formal response blocked until counterpart-office issues are clarified

Interpretation:

- the module skills differ appropriately
- the JPO flavouring does not vary enough
- several scenarios ask the learner to work inside the same narrow family:
  - trilateral coordination
  - counterpart-office clarification
  - terminology / classification / document-standard inconsistency
- some of the revised options are also longer and more processing-heavy than the parallel non-JPO options

Required action:

- broaden the professional range of Module 2 JPO contexts
- shorten over-loaded JPO options
- avoid using trilateral coordination as the default JPO scenario frame where another realistic context could serve the same skill

### 7. Module 4 has the same problem in a different form: some JPO prompts are still too dense, and some unit-level pairings remain too close

**Category:** Prompt And Task Design / Learner Suitability / Variety  
**Criteria:** `5B.1 Level appropriacy` / `5D.3 Calibration` / `5D.4 Choice-list quality` / `5E.3 Variety without chaos`  
**Result:** `Revise`

Evidence from `adv/md/n10/aw-adv_mod4_n10.md`:

- Unit 12:
  - `E3` JPO option and `H3` JPO option are both external-inquiry scenarios built around an international filing update from a counterpart office, with near-identical communicative pressure
- Unit 13 `H3` JPO option:
  - the verification-checklist scenario is professionally valid but too densely packed with training, tooling, and fallback variables for its choice-list role
- Unit 15:
  - `E1` JPO option:
    - rationale for a standardised terminology framework in trilateral examination or coordination work
  - `H3` JPO option:
    - rationale for a shared template for recording trilateral examination comments

Interpretation:

- Unit 13 shows prompt-load inflation
- Unit 12 and Unit 15 show residual within-unit sameness in their JPO E/H pairings
- the issue is not that these contexts are unrealistic
- the issue is that they are too close, too specialised, or too linguistically loaded relative to the learner target and to the parallel non-JPO choices

Required action:

- simplify the densest JPO options
- separate JPO E/H pairings more decisively in Units 12 and 15
- ensure one JPO option does not feel like a narrowed variation of its paired task
