### 2. Unit 18 `D2` depends on the learner preserving their earlier `B6` paragraph, but the carry-forward requirement is implicit rather than instructed where the paragraph is first written

**Severity:** `Should revise`

**Files:**
- `adv/md/final/modules/aw-adv_mod5_n10.md:662`
- `adv/md/final/modules/aw-adv_mod5_n10.md:734`

**Why this matters:**
- Unit 18 is designed as a revision loop: learners write in `B6`, then revisit that writing in `D2`.
- `D2` explicitly instructs learners to return to the paragraph from `B6`, but the earlier task sequence does not clearly signal at the moment of writing that the paragraph will be needed again.
- In self-study or interrupted-use contexts, this increases the chance that the learner treats the `B6` paragraph as disposable and arrives at `D2` without the needed source text.

**Evidence:**
- `D2` says: `Return to the paragraph you wrote in Section B (B6).`
- Later in the same unit, `H2` includes a fallback for learners without workplace documents, showing that accessibility support is already part of this unit's design logic.

**Recommended action:**
- Add a short forward-link note in `B6` telling learners to keep the paragraph for reuse in `D2`, so the revision chain is explicit at the point of production rather than only at the point of reuse.

**Resolution note (2026-03-22):**
- Completed in the live source.
- `B6` now explicitly tells learners to keep the paragraph because it will be reused in `D2`, making the revision chain visible at the point of first production.

### 3. Unit 12 freer writing still sits noticeably below the surrounding Module 4 output range, creating a progression dip inside the live advanced baseline

**Severity:** `Monitor / possible revise`

**Files:**
- `adv/md/final/modules/aw-adv_mod4_n10.md:216`
- `adv/md/final/modules/aw-adv_mod4_n10.md:285`

**Why this matters:**
- Module 4 otherwise works in the `~250-word` band for major output tasks.
- Unit 12 `E3` asks for `180–220` words, while the same unit's `H3` is `220–250` words and the surrounding module-level output expectations sit higher.
- The shorter freer-writing length may be intentional because the inquiry is tightly bounded, but in the finished book it reads as a local progression dip that should be explicitly accepted or normalized rather than left ambiguous.

**Evidence:**
- `E3`: `Write a formal external inquiry of **180–220 words**.`
- `H3`: `Write a **220–250 word** external inquiry...`

**Recommended action:**
- Make an explicit editorial decision: either keep the shorter `E3` as an intentional bounded exception and note that in the planning layer, or bring it into closer alignment with the module's surrounding output range.

**Resolution note (2026-03-22):**
- Decision made: keep the shorter `E3` as an intentional bounded exception.
- The planning layer now records that Unit 12 `E3` stays shorter because it is a tightly bounded inquiry task rather than a broader Module 4 output.
