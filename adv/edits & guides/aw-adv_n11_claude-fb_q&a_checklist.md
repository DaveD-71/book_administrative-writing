## Assessment: N11 QA Checklist and Round 2 Review

### Overall verdict on the checklist

The checklist is **substantially fit for purpose** and significantly better than a typical ELT QA framework would be at this stage. The three-tier structure (Category → Criterion → Check Item), the status vocabulary (`Pass / Revise / Monitor / Deferred`), the evidence rule (4-part `Revise` record), and the source hierarchy in Section 2 are all solid. The coverage across 13 categories (5A–5M) is broad and reflects the real complexity of the project.

That said, there are gaps and weaknesses worth addressing before the next substantive pass.

---

### Where the checklist needs improvement

**1. The H1 Extension Task structural decision is unresolved — but the checklist has no specific check for it.**

The open H1 issue (present in N10 files for Units 2, 3, 4, 6, and 11 where structural profiles say it shouldn't be) is exactly the kind of blocker that N11 should surface. The checklist covers structural profile compliance under 5G.1, but it doesn't name Extension Task compliance specifically. This is a known live issue that should appear in 5G or 5M as a named check, or at minimum as a current `Monitor` item.

**2. The N10 feedback reports (particularly v2 flagged issues) are not referenced anywhere.**

The checklist has no link to the open items from the N10 feedback reports — the content placement error across Units 19–20, the possible missing D3 in Unit 20, and the word count inconsistency in Unit 21. These are pre-existing identified issues. The checklist as written would not necessarily catch them unless the reviewer happens to look at those exact units carefully. A cross-check against the N10 feedback report findings should be an explicit step — either as a preliminary item in Section 4 (Review Workflow) or as named items in 5M (Remaining Pinned-Item Checks).

**3. Section 5C (Language and Learning) is the right area but doesn't have sufficient depth for post-N10 checking.**

The N10 framework has six minimum deliverables for a unit to be considered complete (conceptual framing, "why this works" element, contrast explanation, worked example, bridge to production, and alignment). The N11 checklist checks these in a general way under 5C.1–5C.4, but doesn't ask whether *all six deliverables* are present. A reviewer could mark 5C as `Pass` based on the general impression of a well-explained unit while still missing that one of the six required elements is absent. Given that N10 completeness is exactly what N11 is verifying, the checklist should explicitly reference the N10 minimum deliverables list (Section 9 of the framework) as the concrete standard for 5C.

**4. Activity heading format compliance is not checked.**

The project has an established rule that activity headings must follow the format `#### Activity Name (Code)` — name first, code in brackets — and code-first formats are errors. This is a real and documented standard, but it appears nowhere in 5E (Consistency and Cohesion) or 5G (Structural Profile Compliance) or 5L (Formatting). The Round 2 review found labelling inconsistencies in Modules 1 and 3; a specific check item for heading format would have caught those earlier and more reliably.

**5. The Round 2 review document correctly identifies the active blockers but doesn't close any loops.**

The Round 2 document names five next steps but records no outcomes. It ends with `N11 remains in progress`. At this point, it's more of an interim finding record than a completed review. This is fine as a working document, but it means there's no clear sense of what has been done *since* Round 2. Before N11 can be signed off, there needs to be a Round 3 or a final pass that explicitly revisits each Round 2 `Revise` item and records whether it now `Pass`es.

**6. No check for the learning goals mapping document.**

The `admin-writing-adv_learning-goals-mapping.md` document has 14 flagged issues across five categories that have not yet been actioned. These don't currently appear anywhere in the N11 checklist or the Round 2 review. If those issues are legitimately deferred, they should be named in 5M.2 (Deferred Pinned Items) so they don't disappear.
