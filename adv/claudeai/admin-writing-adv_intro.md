# **Administrative Writing, Advanced**

*(Administrative & Professional Communication Writing, Units 1–23)*

## **a. Purpose of the Text**

The *Administrative Writing, Advanced* textbook develops learners' ability to produce **independent, precise, coherent, and context-appropriate professional writing** suitable for mid-level administrative and operational roles.

It shifts from basic structures to **complex, multi-layered communication**, requiring the learner to synthesise information, justify decisions, summarise meetings and reports, and communicate across multiple stakeholders.

## **b. Target Audience**

* CEFR **B2–B2+** workplace learners
* Administrative officers, team leaders, analysts, and mid-level professionals
* Learners who already understand basic writing forms but must handle:

  * complex explanations
  * multi-stage tasks
  * stakeholder-sensitive communication
  * data-driven summaries
  * professional persuasion

## **c. Learning Objectives**

By the end of the textbook, learners will:

### **Advanced Writing Skills**

* Produce multi-paragraph emails and report sections
* Summarise lengthy or complex documents
* Explain policy changes and procedures with precision
* Provide balanced justification, reasoning, and recommendations
* Write for different stakeholders with different information needs
* Manage tone in sensitive or high-stakes communication

### **Advanced Editing & Revision**

* Edit writing for high-level accuracy and nuance
* Demonstrate strong command of **coherence**, using transitions and logical structure
* Refine tone for formality, diplomacy, and audience appropriateness
* Apply conciseness without losing essential detail

### **Integrated Communication Competence**

* Handle complex, multi-document scenarios
* Write notices, memos, summaries, instructions, and justifications
* Prepare multi-section reports combining narrative, explanation, and recommendations
* Revise and refine a **professional writing portfolio**

## **d. Lesson Activities**

Across 23 units, the textbook includes:

* **Complex scenario-based tasks** requiring synthesis of information
* **Multi-paragraph writing practice** (emails, memos, reports)
* **Analysis of longer texts** for structure and clarity
* **Tone adjustment tasks** for diplomacy, formality, and audience
* **Data interpretation** for writing summaries or updates
* **Extended guided writing** with model structures
* **Editing workshops** (peer-review style tasks)
* **Multi-document case studies**
* **Cumulative portfolio tasks** demonstrating independent writing ability

These activities transform learners from controlled writers into **autonomous professional communicators**.

## **e. How to Use This Book**

### **Group and Self-Study Use**

This textbook is designed for both classroom use and independent self-study. Most activities work in either context without modification.

Where an activity asks you to **discuss** something — with a partner, in a group, or with a colleague — this instruction is intended for learners working in a class or with a study partner. If you are studying independently, treat discussion prompts as **written reflection tasks**: write your response before continuing. This practice mirrors what you will do in the writing tasks that follow and reinforces the same thinking process.

This convention applies throughout the book and is not repeated in individual activity instructions.

### **Activity Types**

Each unit draws on a defined set of activity types. These are consistent across the book, so you will quickly become familiar with what each one involves. The main activity types are:

**Opening & Activation** — Activities that begin each unit. They activate prior knowledge, connect the topic to your professional experience, and prepare you for the model text.

**Model Text Activities** — Activities built around a model text that demonstrates the target writing skill. You will compare weak and strong versions, identify key features, and analyse structure.

**Language Focus & Controlled Practice** — Explanation and practice of specific language, grammar, or structural patterns central to the unit skill. Practice activities are controlled: answers are largely predictable and can be checked against a key.

**Guided Writing** — Structured writing tasks that walk you through the target skill step by step, with prompts and frameworks to support you.

**Freer Writing** — Extended writing tasks where you apply the unit skill independently, with less scaffolding. These are the core production tasks in each unit.

**Peer Review & Self-Assessment** — Structured review activities in which you evaluate a piece of writing against clear criteria.

**Editing & Accuracy Work** — Focused editing tasks: correcting, improving, or making decisions about a text.

**Extension & Homework** — Optional extension tasks and a homework writing assignment for further independent practice.

### **Peer Review Sections**

Each unit includes a peer review section with a structured checklist. In a classroom setting, you exchange writing with a partner and use the checklist to give feedback.

If you are working independently, use the checklist to **review your own writing** before you finish. Read your text as if you were a reader encountering it for the first time. Work through each criterion on the checklist and mark whether your writing meets it. Where it does not, revise before moving on.

Self-review using a structured checklist is itself a professional skill. Practising it independently is as valuable as receiving feedback from another person.

### **Progression Through the Book**

The six modules are designed to be worked through in order. Skills and vocabulary introduced in earlier modules are assumed and built upon in later ones. Within each module, units follow a logical sequence and should generally be completed in order.

Writing tasks increase in length and complexity across the book, from approximately 150 words in Module 1 to 300–350 words in the capstone units (Units 22–23). This progression is deliberate: it reflects the increasing demands placed on professional writers at B2+ level.
