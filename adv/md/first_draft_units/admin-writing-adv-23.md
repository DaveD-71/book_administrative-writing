# MODULE 6. Capstone & Applied Administrative Writing

## Unit 23 — Capstone: Full Administrative Writing Assessment

### Unit Overview

This capstone unit evaluates students’ ability to apply all skills from Units 1–22 in a comprehensive administrative writing task. Students demonstrate mastery in:

- tone control and diplomatic phrasing

- structure selection and coherence

- cohesion and thematic progression

- synthesis of multiple sources

- clarity in explanation, requests, rationales, and recommendations

- audience adaptation

This unit functions as the final assessment for the Upper Intermediate Writing Course.

### Assessment Scenario (Multi-Source Input)

You receive the following:

1. **Email from Office A:** Requests clarification about terminology differences in the revised guidelines; notes confusion among staff.

2. **Email from Office B:** Reports delays caused by outdated forms still in circulation; requests instructions on implementation.

3. **Extract from a meeting summary:** Several offices raised questions about applicant communication timelines; next steps were not assigned.

4. **Updated instructions (last week):** Introduced new terminology, updated steps, new verification requirements; unclear dissemination across offices.

5. **Internal supervisor note:** Asks you to prepare a concise administrative output summarising issues and proposing next steps.

### Warm-Up

#### Activity 1 — Identify All Writing Tasks Hidden in the Scenario

- synthesis of multiple sources

- explanation of issues

- clarification request

- recommendation and rationale

- multi-audience message or report

- next-step planning

#### Activity 2 — Who Are the Possible Audiences?

Consider:

- internal supervisor

- international partner offices

- mixed audience (multi-office distribution)

Which type of communication best fits each?

### Model Text (Capstone Example)

> *"This report summarises the current implementation challenges arising from the revised applicant communication guidelines. Offices A and C reported uncertainty regarding the updated terminology, while Office B noted delays due to continued use of outdated forms. Additionally, questions remain regarding the appropriate sequence for applicant communication steps. To address these issues, we propose circulating a clarification sheet summarising key terminology, confirming whether all offices have received the updated instructions, and revising the implementation schedule as needed. Once clarification is complete, offices will be better positioned to adopt the updated workflow uniformly."*

#### Activity — Analyse the Model

Identify how it demonstrates:

- synthesis of multiple sources

- tone control

- clear structure

- clear next steps

- administrative usefulness

### Language Focus

#### 1. Selecting the Most Appropriate Output Type

**Writing goal:** Match purpose, content, and audience to the most effective structure.

Possible formats:

- consolidated report

- administrative notice

- multi-audience message

- executive brief

- clarification request

#### Practice A — Match Output to Purpose

Match:

1. Need to summarise issues for a supervisor → ?

2. Need to request clarification about terminology → ?

3. Need to inform all offices about required next steps → ?

4. Need to explain delays and propose solutions → ?

#### 2. Demonstrating Complete Synthesis

**Writing goal:** Avoid listing; instead, combine inputs into themes.

**Example:**
*Two main issues are evident across all sources: unclear terminology and inconsistent use of forms.*

#### Practice B — Group Ideas into Themes

Group:

1. *Terminology confusion*  

2. *Old forms in use*  

3. *Timeline questions*  

4. *Terminology unclear in multiple emails*  

5. *Timeline issues raised in meeting*

#### 3. Signalling Next Steps Clearly

**Writing goal:** Ensure that the administrative output supports immediate follow-up.

**Example:**
*The next step is to confirm receipt of updated instructions and circulate clarifications.*

#### Practice C — Add Next-Step Information

Rewrite:

1. *Offices have many questions.*

### Freer Writing

#### Task — Capstone Output (15–20 sentences)

Write a fully integrated administrative document of your chosen type. It must:

- synthesise all scenario inputs

- present issues + impacts

- clarify or request information as needed

- propose solutions or next steps

- use clear, professional structure

- demonstrate mastery of the entire course skill set

#### Peer Review Questions

- Does the writing integrate all sources without listing them?

- Is the tone appropriately formal and diplomatic?

- Are issues and actions presented clearly?

- Does structure support readability and decision-making?

### Editing Skills

#### Editing Checklist

Check:

- Is tone neutral throughout?

- Are any points repeated unnecessarily?

- Are themes and issues synthesised effectively?

- Are next steps realistic and clear?

- Is the document format appropriate for the chosen audience?

#### Editing Exercise — Refine the Capstone Output

Rewrite:
*"There are many problems in the offices and things are not working well. Someone needs to explain everything again."*

Improve by:

- identifying issues clearly

- synthesising information

- adding next steps

- adopting formal tone

### Extension Task

Choose one:

1. Convert the capstone output into a shorter executive summary.

2. Rewrite your capstone document for an international audience.

3. Expand your capstone into a full consolidated report.

### Homework

Write a **300–350 word** capstone administrative output using the scenario package. Your writing must:

- synthesise the five inputs

- present issues, impacts, and themes

- recommend or request actions

- outline next steps

- demonstrate full command of tone and structure.
